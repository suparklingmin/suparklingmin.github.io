I am **Sumin Park**, a computational linguist. I am currently a visiting assistant professor at the College of Humanities, Seoul National University, where I teach in the [Data Science for the Humanities](https://dsh.snu.ac.kr) interdisciplinary program.

## Research Interests

### Sentence embeddings

As a computational linguist and NLP researcher, I work on methods that **represent the meaning of a sentence as a vector**, such as [KR-SBERT](https://huggingface.co/snunlp/KR-SBERT-V40K-klueNLI-augSTS). I am especially interested in two questions:

- embedding models that can capture when sentences of the same form are used with different meanings within a larger context
- measures that reflect not only the semantic similarity between two texts but also their relatedness at other levels, such as pragmatics and stylistics

### Linguistic variation

As a linguist, I model **linguistic variation** quantitatively and apply it to a range of texts. I describe, through frequency, co-occurrence, and distribution in corpora, how the same referent, meaning, or function is realized differently under different conditions — reaching beyond literal meaning into the pragmatic, stylistic, and social.

- **Variation across time (diachronic)**: lexical and semantic change in historical Korean and historical corpora (e.g. [the taste adjective *dalda* "sweet" in late-Joseon cookbooks](/ko/research/dalda/), [the character *鬼* in the *Veritable Records of the Joseon Dynasty*](/ko/research/gwi/))
- **Variation across social conditions (synchronic)**: differences by speaker–addressee relation, medium, and readership (e.g. gendered [X-男]/[X-女] references in news headlines; speakers' self-reference terms in early Chinese histories)
